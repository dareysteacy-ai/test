Keeping Meaning in Order: Summarize (Student Practice)

Practice summarizing by keeping the meaning the same and the ideas in order.

Directions Do your best

Choose a passage. Then complete the activities. When you click Score, you’ll get feedback right away.

  • A summary tells the key ideas in fewer words.
  • Keep the meaning the same.
  • Keep ideas in logical order (first → next → last).
Tip: Read the whole passage once before answering.

Passage

Help & Supports (Differentiation) ⌄

Use these supports if you need them (they match the differentiation categories used in the model lesson).

  • Below Level: Focus on the topic + 2 key ideas. Use the sentence frame provided.
  • Grade Level: Summarize with topic + 3 key ideas in order.
  • Above Level: Write both a paraphrase of one sentence and a summary of the whole passage; explain the difference.

Sentence Frames

  • Summary frame: “This text is mostly about ___. First ___. Then ___. Finally ___.”
  • One-sentence summary: “The passage explains that ___.”
